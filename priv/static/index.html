Why You Should Join?

. . . . . . . . . .

You may have seen a machine in a window of the 1000 building at Techwood or its baby brother on 10S at CNN and wondered who uses it and why.

Guess what? It’s YOU.

Matthew Drooker, Maigh Houlihan, Rahul Rawla and a few others on our tech team are going to pull out the guts of these guys and rewire them so you can walk up to it and get what you need (in some TBD fashion, maybe swipe your badge, put in your employee ID or maybe talk to it).If you’ve ever dabbled with Arduino, RaspberryPi, Node, Mongo, or a soldering iron — or wanted to — now is your chance; we need you! If you’re an aspiring mobile designer or web developer, we need those skills, too.

We’ll be working on the project after-hours, so this is a completely voluntary, but very fun and rewarding project. Shoot Maigh an email if you’re interested in coming to out to play and learn.

We’ll kick things off in July and will communicate more as we get closer. Stay tuned!

How does a hackathon work?

Hackathons provide a venue for self-expression and creativity through technology. People with technical backgrounds come together, form teams around a problem or idea, and collaboratively code a unique solution from scratch — these generally take shape in the form of websites, mobile apps, and robots.

Frequently Asked Questions

. . . . . . . . . .

Answers to commonly asked questions.

1

I have never been to a hackathon before, what should I do?

No worries at all. We will be providing mentors during the hackathon and running workshops. You can even read on one of our execs' experience when she started here to see how easy and fun it really is to learn at the hackathon.

2

What should I bring?

You will definitely need your laptop, phone, their chargers, as well as a valid student or government ID. Feel free to also bring along a sleeping bag, and we recommend some comfortable clothes.

3

How about teams?

Once the idea generation is done, there will be an open mic and networking time to find a team. Everyone accepted will also be invited to the DeltaHacks' Slack prior to the hackathon in order to discuss ideas.

4

How much does it cost?

Nothing! Zero! The event is absolutely free for all participants. We will provide you with the venue, food, Wi-Fi, etc. You just need to show up.
